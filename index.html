    Brief History of Chess.

    Chess originated in ancient India around the 6th century AD, where it was known as "Chaturanga". Initially, Chaturanga featured four military divisions represented by so called pieces to modern-day rooks, knights, bishops, and kings. The game spread across Persia, where it evolved into "Shatranj". Shatranj introduced new pieces like the queen and pawns, while the game's strategy and rules continued to develop. In the 10th century, chess reached Europe and the rules underwent further refinement, leading to the emergence of modern chess during the Renaissance. Significant changes included the powerful queen and pawn advancements, transforming the game into a dynamic contest of strategy and foresight. The 19th century saw the rise of some of the most legendary players and the advent of World Chess Championships. With the development of computers, chess entered a new era, and today it remains a global sport with millions competing online and in tournaments worldwide.
